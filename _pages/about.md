I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Information Systems at the W. P. Carey School of Business, Arizona State University, advised by Professor **[Pei-yu Chen](https://search.asu.edu/profile/2217544)** and **[Raghu Santanam](https://search.asu.edu/profile/192381)**.

My research investigates how emerging technologies—such as **AI, Generative AI, digital platforms, and in-store technologies**—reshape consumer behavior, firm strategies, and societal outcomes. I focus on three major areas:  

- **Traditional online and offline grocery retail**, including innovations such as **Home Delivery Services** and **Unmanned Systems**.  

- **Automobile industry**, examining the entire consumer journey from **online lead generation** (e.g., *TikTok/Kuaishou livestreaming, Autohome advertising*) to **AI-driven invitations to stores** (e.g., *AI diagnostic systems*), to **in-store visits and consumer–sales consultant interactions** (increasingly supported by **LLM/GenAI systems**), and ultimately to **test drives and final purchases**.  

- **AI-enabled persuasion and human–AI interaction**, focusing on how **LLM-generated persuasive communication** influences consumer decision-making. In ongoing work, I study **charitable giving contexts**, examining how AI-generated messages affect donation behavior, message receptivity, and trust in human–AI interaction.  

Methodologically, I leverage econometric and statistical analyses, field and lab experiments, and machine learning/deep learning to design studies, analyze data, infer causality, and evaluate managerial policies.  

I also develop **AI-based experimental artifacts**, including LLM-powered persuasion systems and interactive agent interfaces built from scratch using Python. My work covers end-to-end implementation, from front-end interface design to back-end logic, data collection, and the development of reusable research tools.  

Before my Ph.D., I studied at the Research Centre on Fictitious Economy and Data Science, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and the School of Economics and Management, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, where I developed interdisciplinary training in economics, computer science, and data science.
